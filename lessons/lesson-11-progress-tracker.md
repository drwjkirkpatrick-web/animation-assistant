## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this lesson, the learner should be able to:
1. Explain how a level and badge system tracks growth in animation skill and why progress must be earned, not handed out.
2. Create a personal progress tracker for their animation work and accurately self-assess which badges they have earned based on evidence in their projects.
3. Value honest self-assessment and connect steady practice to long-term improvement.

## Key Inquiry Question

> "How does a green sea turtle (*kasa*) grow steadily over its lifetime — and how can we track our own animation growth with levels and badges, just like a turtle slowly but surely glides forward?"

## Local Context: Kenyan Ocean Animal

**Animal:** Green Sea Turtle (Swahili: *Kasa*)  
**Habitat:** Seagrass beds in Watamu Marine National Park, Diani Reef, and Malindi Marine National Park. Green sea turtles graze on seagrass and nest on Kenya's beaches.  
**Why this animal?** The green sea turtle grows slowly and steadily over a long lifetime — it can live 60-70 years and takes decades to reach maturity. This makes it the perfect metaphor for animation progress: growth takes time, patience, and consistent practice. The turtle doesn't rush; it glides forward, and so does a young animator building skills level by level. In Kenyan culture, the turtle (*kasa*) is seen as patient and wise — qualities that align with the growth mindset this lesson teaches.  
**Conservation note:** The green sea turtle is **Endangered** (IUCN Red List). Major threats include plastic pollution (turtles mistake plastic bags for jellyfish), bycatch in fishing nets, and habitat loss from coastal development. **KWS protects turtle nesting sites at Watamu** — rangers monitor beaches at night during nesting season, relocate eggs to safe enclosures, and release hatchlings into the ocean. Local conservation groups like Local Ocean Conservation (based in Watamu) also rescue injured turtles. When we use the turtle as our progress mascot, we also remember why protecting them matters.

## Lesson Development

### Introduction (8 min)

Show a photo or short video of a green sea turtle (*kasa*) gliding through seagrass. Ask: "How long do you think a sea turtle lives? How long does it take to grow up?"

Share: A green sea turtle can live 60-70 years and takes 20-30 years to reach adulthood. It grows slowly, steadily, and patiently.

Now connect: "Animation skill grows the same way. You won't become a master animator in one day. But if you track your progress — level by level, badge by badge — you'll see how far you've come. Today we're building a **progress tracker** that helps you see your growth, just like a turtle slowly gliding forward."

Ask: "Who remembers what animation projects you've completed so far?" Let learners list them. Explain that today they will look at their own work and figure out which badges they've earned.

### Step 1: Concept Introduction — Levels, Badges & Honest Assessment (15 min)

Introduce the **6-level system** from the progress tracker module. Display it on the screen:

| Level | Name | What It Means | Badge Examples |
|-------|------|---------------|----------------|
| 1 | First Frame 🐣 | Complete your first animation | "I Made Something Move," "Tool Explorer," "First Export" |
| 2 | Ball Buster ⚽ | Master bouncing ball fundamentals | "Squash Master," "Arc Archer," "Timing Keeper," "Weight Lifter" |
| 3 | Body Mechanic 🚶 | Walk cycles and body mechanics | "Walk Cycle Warrior," "Anticipation Ace," "Follow-Through Phenom" |
| 4 | Performer 🎭 | Emotion and character | "Mood Master," "Eye Contact," "Gesture Genius" |
| 5 | Storyteller 📖 | Multi-shot stories | "Storyboard Sketcher," "Cut Master," "Short Film Finisher" |
| 6 | Master Animator 🏆 | Advanced technique | "Rigging Rookie," "Effect Animator," "Mentor Badge" |

**The key rule:** Badges must be **earned, not handed out.** If your work doesn't show a principle, you don't get the badge yet — and that's okay. The turtle doesn't rush. You say: "Keep working on [principle] and you'll earn [badge]!"

**How to assess honestly:** For each badge, look at your actual projects and ask:
- *Squash Master:* Can I point to a frame where the ball visibly squashes on impact? If yes — earned. If no — not yet.
- *Arc Archer:* Does my ball follow a curved arc, not a V-shape? If yes — earned.
- *Timing Keeper:* Does the ball slow at the top and speed up falling? If yes — earned.

Show learners the **text-based progress bar** they will use:

```
Level 2: ██████░░░░ 60% (3/5 badges for Level 3)
```

Explain: Each level requires earning a set number of badges. Fill in the bar based on what you've actually earned.

**Badge Tracker Sheet Template (print one per learner):**

```

Current Level: ___  Mascot: 🐢 Kasa the Turtle

[ ] I Made Something Move  — Evidence: _______________
[ ] Tool Explorer (tried 2 tools) — Evidence: __________
[ ] First Export — Evidence: ________________________

[ ] Squash Master — Evidence: ______________________
[ ] Arc Archer — Evidence: _________________________
[ ] Timing Keeper — Evidence: _____________________
[ ] Weight Lifter — Evidence: ______________________
```

### Step 2: Guided Practice — Assessing a Sample Project Together (12 min)

Show a sample animation on the screen — use the **jellyfish squash & stretch** animation from Lesson 09, or any completed class project.

Walk through the assessment together:

**Teacher:** "Let's look at this jellyfish animation. Which badges could this earn?"

**Ask the class:**
1. "Did the jellyfish visibly squash and stretch?" → If yes: **Squash Master** badge earned. (Evidence: "Frame 5 — bell is flattened and wider.")
2. "Did it follow an arc path?" → The jellyfish moves up and down, not in an arc. → **Arc Archer** NOT earned yet. "Keep working on arc paths and you'll earn it!"
3. "Did it slow at the top and speed up falling?" → Check the timing. If the `wait` times are longer at the top: **Timing Keeper** earned.
4. "Is this the learner's first animation?" → If yes: **"I Made Something Move"** earned!

Fill in the badge tracker on the board together:

```
Level 1: ██████████ 100% (3/3 — all Level 1 badges earned!)
Level 2: █████░░░░░ 50% (2/4 — Squash Master + Timing Keeper)
```

Emphasize: "We didn't give the Arc Archer badge because the jellyfish doesn't move in an arc. That's honest. The turtle is patient — you'll earn it when you do an arc challenge."

**Scratch code for a digital progress bar** (optional, for advanced learners who want to build a tracker in Scratch):

```scratch
when [green flag] clicked
set [badges_earned] to (0)
set [badges_needed] to (4)
ask [Did your ball squash on impact? (yes/no)] and wait
if <(answer) = [yes]> then
  change [badges_earned] by (1)
  say [Squash Master badge earned!] for (2) seconds
end
ask [Did your ball follow a curved arc? (yes/no)] and wait
if <(answer) = [yes]> then
  change [badges_earned] by (1)
  say [Arc Archer badge earned!] for (2) seconds
end
ask [Did the ball slow at the top and speed up falling? (yes/no)] and wait
if <(answer) = [yes]> then
  change [badges_earned] by (1)
  say [Timing Keeper badge earned!] for (2) seconds
end
// Show progress
set [progress_percent] to (((badges_earned) / (badges_needed)) * (100))
say (join [You're at ] (join (progress_percent) [% toward Level 3!])) for (3) seconds
```

### Step 3: Independent Practice — Build Your Own Tracker (20 min)

Learners fill out their **Badge Tracker Sheet** using their own completed projects.

**Steps:**
1. List all animation projects you have completed (from this class or on your own).
2. For each badge, look at your actual work and write **evidence** — the specific frame, project name, or observation that proves you earned it.
3. If you can't find evidence, leave the badge unchecked. Write what you'd need to do to earn it.
4. Fill in your progress bar.
5. Write the **next badge** you're working toward and what you need to do to earn it.

**For learners who have completed few projects:** They may earn Level 1 badges ("I Made Something Move," "Tool Explorer"). That's a great start! The turtle started small too.

**For advanced learners with many projects:** They may be at Level 2 or 3. Challenge them to identify which Level 3 badges they're closest to and plan a project to earn one.

Walk around and check:
- Are learners providing **specific evidence**, not just checking boxes? (Evidence should name a project and a specific observation.)
- Is anyone giving themselves badges they haven't earned? Gently correct: "I don't see the squash in this one — let's plan to work on that next."

### Step 4: Sharing & Feedback (10 min)

Learners pair up and exchange Badge Tracker Sheets. Each partner:
1. Checks one badge their partner marked as earned. Looks at the evidence. Asks: "Can you show me in your animation?"
2. Suggests one badge their partner could earn next and what project would earn it.

Select 2-3 learners to share their progress bar with the whole class:

> "I'm at Level 2, 60%. I've earned Squash Master and Timing Keeper. I'm working toward Arc Archer — I need to animate something that follows a curved path, like a dolphin jumping."

Celebrate all progress — even Level 1 with one badge. "You made something move. That's the first step every animator takes."

End with the turtle metaphor: "The *kasa* doesn't rush to adulthood. It takes 20-30 years. You don't need to rush to Level 6. Keep practicing, keep tracking, and you'll get there."

## Assessment

| Criteria | BE (Below Expectation) | AE (Approaching) | ME (Meets) | EE (Exceeds) |
|----------|------------------------|-------------------|------------|--------------|
| Badge self-assessment accuracy | Checks badges with no evidence or checks all badges regardless of work | Checks some badges with evidence but marks others without proof | Checks badges only where specific evidence is provided; leaves unearned badges unchecked with a plan | All badges have specific evidence; unearned badges have a concrete plan for what project would earn them |
| Progress tracker completion | Tracker is incomplete or progress bar is not filled in | Tracker is filled in but progress bar is inaccurate (doesn't match badges) | Tracker complete with accurate progress bar and next-badge goal | Tracker complete, accurate, AND includes a planned project with timeline for the next badge |
| Understanding growth mindset | Cannot explain why badges must be earned | Says "it's more fun to earn them" but not why honest assessment matters | Explains that honest tracking shows real growth and that unearned badges are goals, not failures | Explains growth mindset AND connects it to the turtle metaphor (slow, steady, patient growth over time) |

## Extended Activity

**Home Project:** Choose one badge you have NOT earned yet. Create a small animation project specifically designed to earn that badge. Bring it to the next class along with your updated Badge Tracker Sheet.

**Mentor opportunity:** If you've reached Level 2 or higher, help a classmate who is working on a Level 1 or 2 badge. Helping someone else earns you the **Mentor Badge** at Level 6 — but you can start being a mentor at any level!

**Conservation connection:** The green sea turtle (*kasa*) takes decades to grow up — and so do conservation efforts. Research one thing KWS does to protect turtles at Watamu (nesting protection, beach patrols, rescue). Write 2-3 sentences: How is long-term conservation like long-term animation practice? (Both require patience, consistency, and tracking progress over time.)
