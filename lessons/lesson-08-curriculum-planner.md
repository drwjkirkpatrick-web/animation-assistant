## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this lesson, the learner should be able to:
1. Describe the four phases of an animation learning journey — Foundations, Body Mechanics, Performance, Story — and which animation principles belong to each (knowledge)
2. Build a personalized week-by-week learning plan tailored to their own age, experience, tool, and goal, using a Kenyan ocean animal as the project through-line (skill/practical)
3. Take ownership of their own learning path and set realistic, motivating milestones (value/attitude)

## Key Inquiry Question

> If Papa Shinga the whale shark can cross the whole Indian Ocean on a slow, steady journey, what would *your* animation journey look like — from your first bouncing ball to your finished short film?

## Local Context: Kenyan Ocean Animal

**Animal:** Whale Shark — Swahili: *Papa Shinga*  
**Habitat:** Open ocean off **Watamu** and the **Diani-Chale Marine Reserve**; visits the Kenyan coast seasonally (October–February) to feed on plankton  
**Why this animal?** The whale shark is the **largest fish in the ocean** — yet it moves slowly, steadily, and patiently. It crosses vast distances on a long journey, never rushing. This makes Papa Shinga the perfect metaphor for a **learning journey**: big goals reached through calm, steady, week-by-week progress. The whale shark also teaches **weight and scale through timing** — the slow drift of a giant — which anchors the Foundations phase of the curriculum.  
**Conservation note:** Whale sharks are **Endangered** (IUCN). Main threats: boat strikes and fishing. KWS runs a **tagging program** and has issued tourism guidelines so whale-shark-watching boats don't crowd or harm them. By choosing Papa Shinga as their journey mascot, learners connect their own growth to the protection of this gentle giant.

## Lesson Development

### Introduction (8 min)

Show a 30-second clip of a whale shark (papa shinga) gliding slowly through open water, tiny fish swimming alongside it.

Ask: *"How long do you think it takes this whale shark to cross the Indian Ocean?"* (Answer: months — slow and steady.) *"It doesn't sprint. It just keeps going. That's what learning animation is like."*

Introduce the lesson: **Today you don't animate — you plan. You build your own journey from where you are now to the animation you dream of making.** Every great animator had a path. Today you draw yours.

### Step 1: Concept Introduction — The Four-Phase Framework (12 min)

Teach the curriculum framework from Prompt 08. Display the four phases:

```
Your Animation Journey — 4 Phases (8 weeks)

PHASE 1: FOUNDATIONS          (Weeks 1-2)
  Principle focus: squash & stretch, timing, arcs
  Project: bouncing ball / whale-shark glide
  You'll know it's working when: the ball feels heavy

PHASE 2: BODY MECHANICS        (Weeks 3-4)
  Principle focus: follow-through, overlapping action, anticipation, slow in/out
  Project: walk cycle / turtle swim cycle / head turn
  You'll know it's working when: movement feels natural, not robotic

PHASE 3: PERFORMANCE           (Weeks 5-6)
  Principle focus: appeal, staging, exaggeration
  Project: character jump / emotion exercise / lip-sync basics
  You'll know it's working when: your character seems to FEEL something

PHASE 4: STORY                 (Weeks 7-8)
  Principle focus: staging, secondary action, pacing
  Project: storyboard → multi-shot scene → exported short film
  You'll know it's working when: someone watches it and reacts
```

Explain: the plan **adapts to you**. Younger learners (10-12) use Scratch and Stop Motion with playful projects. Teens (14-17) can compress phases and use pro tools like Blender. The time you have per week changes how big each project is — but the phases stay the same.

### Step 2: Guided Practice — Build Papa Shinga's Journey Together (15 min)

Give every learner a **Learner Profile form** to fill in (this is the input the curriculum planner needs):

```
LEARNER PROFILE — "My Animation Journey"

Name: ___________________   Age: _____
Experience:  ( ) none   ( ) some   ( ) intermediate
Tool I want to use: _______________  (or "recommend one for me")
My goal — what I want to eventually make:
_______________________________________________________
(e.g., "a short film about a whale shark," "a funny cartoon,"
 "a stop-motion LEGO space movie," "an anime-style fight scene")
Time per week I can spend: _____ hours
How many weeks I want to learn: _____ (default 8)
```

Now, **together**, build a sample journey on the board for a fictional learner:

> *Amani, age 13, some experience, wants to make "a short film about a whale shark in Watamu," 3 hours/week, 8 weeks.*

Walk through how the planner fills in the **Journey Map** week by week, tying each week to a principle and a whale-shark-themed project:

```
Sample Journey — Amani (age 13, goal: whale shark short film)

Week 1: Foundations I     | Principle: timing & weight
  Project: whale shark glide animation (Scratch glide blocks)
  Milestone: Papa Shinga moves slowly = feels huge

Week 2: Foundations II    | Principle: squash & stretch, arcs
  Project: jellyfish pulse + whale shark passes by
  Milestone: two animals, different weights, same scene

Week 3: Body Mechanics I  | Principle: follow-through
  Project: turtle (kasa) swim cycle with trailing flippers
  Milestone: flippers keep moving after the body turns

Week 4: Body Mechanics II | Principle: anticipation
  Project: dolphin (pomboo) gathers before a jump
  Milestone: clear "wind-up" before the action

Week 5: Performance       | Principle: appeal & staging
  Project: whale shark character — give it personality
  Milestone: the shark feels like a character, not a shape

Week 6: Performance II    | Principle: exaggeration
  Project: octopus (pweza) reacts to the whale shark — emotion!
  Milestone: readable emotion through pose & timing

Week 7: Story I           | Principle: staging
  Project: storyboard the short film (6 panels)
  Milestone: a clear beginning, middle, end on paper

Week 8: Story II          | Principle: secondary action, pacing
  Project: animate & export the finished short
  Milestone: a shareable video — "Papa Shinga's Journey"
```

Point out how the **goal shapes the plan**: Amani's whale-shark goal means every project features Kenyan ocean animals, building toward the final film.

### Step 3: Independent Practice — Design Your Own Journey (20 min)

Learners fill in their own Learner Profile, then design their **personal Journey Map** on the worksheet (8-week grid, or fewer if they chose a shorter duration).

Requirements for each week on their map:
- **Week N: [Theme]**
- **Principle focus:** which of the 12 principles
- **Project:** specific thing they'll make (must include at least one Kenyan ocean animal)
- **Tool:** which software they'll use
- **Time estimate:** how many hours this week
- **Milestone:** what they'll have at the end of the week

**Challenge:** At the bottom, write **3 "progress markers"** — "You'll know you're getting it when…"

**Teacher circulates** and helps learners who:
- Have no goal → offer example goals to choose from (a funny cartoon, a stop-motion LEGO movie, a short film about a Kenyan reef animal)
- Picked a tool too advanced for their age → gently redirect (e.g., a 10-year-old wanting Blender → suggest Scratch now, Blender at 14)
- Set too big a final project → apply the "2x rule": whatever they think it'll take, it'll take twice as long, so simplify

### Step 4: Sharing & Feedback (10 min)

Learners pair up and **pitch their journey** to a partner in 60 seconds: *"I'm going from _____ to _____ in 8 weeks. My final project is _____."* The partner asks one encouraging question.

Two or three learners share with the whole class. Celebrate the variety of goals — every journey is different, and that's the point.

Display all Journey Maps on a "Our Animation Journeys" wall.

## Assessment

| Criteria | BE (Below Expectation) | AE (Approaching) | ME (Meets) | EE (Exceeds) |
|----------|------------------------|-------------------|------------|--------------|
| Understands the 4 phases | Cannot name the phases | Names 1-2 phases with help | Names all 4 phases and matches principles to them | Explains why the phases are ordered that way; can reorder for a compressed timeline |
| Builds a complete journey plan | Plan is blank or has 1-2 weeks | Plan covers most weeks but missing principle/milestone | Complete 8-week (or chosen duration) plan: theme + principle + project + milestone per week | Plan is complete, goal-tailored, realistic (2x rule), AND includes 3+ progress markers |
| Takes ownership & sets realistic goals | Copies the sample plan unchanged | Personalizes the goal but projects are too big/unrealistic | Own goal, age-appropriate tool, realistic weekly time budget | Plan adapts tool progression over time + anticipates which week will be hardest and why |

## Extended Activity

**Homework — Start Week 1**
Your journey begins now. Complete your **Week 1 project** before the next class (the Foundations project from your plan). Bring the result — even if it's rough. *Papa Shinga didn't cross the ocean in a day.*

**Conservation connection:** For learners whose final project features a Kenyan marine animal, require one **conservation fact** in the finished film (endangered status + one KWS action). The animation journey ends with a story that matters.

## Differentiation

| For learners who need more support | For advanced learners (13-17) |
|-------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Provide a **partially-filled Journey Map** with themes and principles pre-written; learners only fill in the project and milestone. Offer a menu of 5 example goals to pick from instead of writing their own. | Have advanced learners build a **personalized plan generator** in Python that takes a learner profile and prints a week-by-week plan — exactly what the AI curriculum-planner module does: |

```python
# Animation Journey Planner — generates a personalized learning plan
# Ages 13+: this is the logic behind the curriculum-planner assistant.
# Run:  python3 journey_planner.py

def build_journey(name, age, experience, goal, weeks=8, hours_per_week=3):
    """Build a week-by-week animation learning plan."""

    # The 4-phase framework, each phase = list of (theme, principle, project)
    phases = {
        "Foundations": [
            ("Foundations I",  "timing & weight",        "whale shark glide (papa shinga)"),
            ("Foundations II", "squash & stretch, arcs", "jellyfish pulse + shark passes by"),
        ],
        "Body Mechanics": [
            ("Body Mechanics I",  "follow-through",      "turtle swim cycle (kasa)"),
            ("Body Mechanics II", "anticipation, slow in/out", "dolphin jump wind-up (pomboo)"),
        ],
        "Performance": [
            ("Performance I",  "appeal & staging",   "give your animal a personality"),
            ("Performance II", "exaggeration",       "octopus reacts with emotion (pweza)"),
        ],
        "Story": [
            ("Story I", "staging",        "storyboard your goal project (6 panels)"),
            ("Story II","pacing, secondary action", "animate & export your finished short"),
        ],
    }

    # Adapt: younger learners get playful Scratch projects; teens go pro
    tool = "Scratch / Stop Motion Studio" if age <= 12 else "Krita / Blender (your choice)"

    # Compress if fewer weeks requested
    week_list = []
    w = 1
    for phase_name, weeks_data in phases.items():
        for theme, principle, project in weeks_data:
            if w > weeks:
                break
            week_list.append({
                "week": w,
                "theme": theme,
                "principle": principle,
                "project": project + f"  [goal tie-in: {goal}]",
                "tool": tool,
                "hours": hours_per_week,
            })
            w += 1

    return week_list

# --- Try it: Amani's whale-shark journey ---
my_plan = build_journey(
    name="Amani", age=13, experience="some",
    goal="a short film about a whale shark in Watamu",
    weeks=8, hours_per_week=3,
)

print("=== AMANI'S ANIMATION JOURNEY ===\n")
for wk in my_plan:
    print(f"Week {wk['week']}: {wk['theme']}")
    print(f"  Principle: {wk['principle']}")
    print(f"  Project:   {wk['project']}")
    print(f"  Tool:      {wk['tool']}  |  Time: {wk['hours']}h")
    print(f"  Milestone: have a working {wk['theme'].split()[0].lower()} demo\n")

print("PROGRESS MARKERS:")
print("  1. You'll know timing when your whale shark feels HEAVY and slow.")
print("  2. You'll know follow-through when flippers trail after a turn.")
print("  3. You'll know staging when a viewer's eye goes where you want.")
print("\nPapa Shinga says: Kila safari huanza na hatua moja. (Every journey starts with one step.)")
```
