# Kenyan Indian Ocean Marine Animals — Animation Reference

This document catalogs marine animals found in Kenya's Indian Ocean waters,
organized by how useful they are as animation subjects. Use these animals in
lesson plans so learners connect animation concepts to their local environment.

---

## Ocean Motion Categories

### 1. SMOOTH SWIMMING (Arcs + Timing)
Best animals: **Green Sea Turtle, Angelfish, Butterflyfish**

These animals glide with steady, graceful arcs. Perfect for teaching:
- Arcs principle (flippers trace wide curves)
- Slow-in/slow-out (swimming has natural acceleration/deceleration)
- Weight (a turtle is heavy, a butterflyfish is light)

### 2. DARTING & DIRECTION CHANGES (Anticipation + Squash/Stretch)
Best animals: **Clownfish, Wrasse, Triggerfish**

These fish zip and turn suddenly. Perfect for teaching:
- Anticipation (slight pull-back before darting forward)
- Squash & stretch (body compresses on the turn, stretches on the dart)
- Staging (camera follows the action)

### 2. SECONDARY MOTION (Follow-Through + Overlap)
Best animals: **Lionfish, Jellyfish, Octopus, Cuttlefish**

These animals have parts that trail behind the body. Perfect for teaching:
- Follow-through (fins/tentacles lag behind body direction change)
- Overlapping action (tentacles don't all move at the same time)
- Delay principle (secondary motion starts AFTER primary motion)

### 4. WEIGHT & SCALE (Timing + Spacing)
Best animals: **Whale Shark, Humpback Whale, Dugong**

These are the ocean giants. Perfect for teaching:
- Scale through timing (whale moves slowly = feels huge)
- Weight (heavy impact, slow recovery)
- The Odd Rule (falling/rising through water column)

### 5. BREACHING & JUMPING (Anticipation + Arcs + Impact)
Best animals: **Humpback Whale, Dolphin, Manta Ray**

These animals leave the water. Perfect for teaching:
- Anticipation (deep dive before the breach)
- Arc trajectory through air
- Splash impact (water reaction = action/reaction)
- Weight contrast (heavy in air vs. buoyant in water)

### 6. BOTTOM-DWELLING & CRAWLING (Contact + Weight Shift)
Best animals: **Octopus, Moray Eel, Sea Turtle (on land), Crab**

These animals interact with the ocean floor. Perfect for teaching:
- Ground contact (feet/suction cups plant and grip)
- Weight shift (octopus shifting between arms)
- Surface interaction (leaving trails, disturbing sand)
